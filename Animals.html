VR Animals

A VR Application Helping Students Learn about Animals

About

VR Animals is an independent side project of mine providing an immersive, intimate, intriguing and educational experience to help students learn about animals. It enables students to observe vivid 3D animal models as close as they want and learn knowledge about them. Besides, students can interact with animals using controllers including controlling them to move and even touching them. Moreover, students can create their own animal world in the app by placing animals they like in the environment. Currently, it's at the prototyping stage and based on the hardware platform of Oculus.

My Role

Independent project - Product Design, Interaction/Visual Design, Animation Design, Prototyping

Tools

Demo Video

The Problem

To learn knowledge about animals is an important part of students' biology education. Also, young students and children tend to have a natural interest in wild animals and hope to have a more intimate contact with them. However, due to safety and hygiene concerns, there are very few intimate contacts can be allowed between students and animals in traditional biology education including watching videos, reading textbooks and observing specimen, which are usually tedious and lacking interactions.

Solution

The development of virtual reality has opened a new door for education, which is especially true for biology. By bringing students into the immersive virtual world of animals, they can get to animals as close as they want like never before. They can observe details of animals from every angle without any safety or hygiene concern. Thus, I designed this VR education for students, the "VR Animals".

"VR Animals" is built on the VR platform of Oculus Rift, which utilizes input sources including the locomotion of headset, two 6 DOF touch controllers and voice command to provide an interactive and intriguing biology education experience to students.

Core Features and Interactions

Main Menu

In the main menu of the application, students can choose to go to different worlds to see different animals. Animals of the same general category will be living in the same world. For now, there are mammal island and undersea world with mammals and sea animals respectively.

Animal Map







After entering the animal world, students will be in an environment with animals randomly spawned in different places. They will need to explore the environment in search for animals. The animal map serves to help them navigate in the world and find animals.

Control Animals






In the animal world, animals will walk around like in the real world. Once students spot an animal, if they point the animal with the laser pointer, the animal will stop moving. Then they can use the thumbstick on their left controller to control the movement of the animal.

Animal Introduction

For any animal selected close enough to the student, they can open the introduction panel using the button on the controller. This introduction panel including text introduction, a 360° video about the animal image search results from Google.

Touch Animals





Students can also touch animals close to them to trigger animations. Moreover, there will be haptic feedback from the touching together with the animation.

Create Your Own Animal World




Other than visiting the built-in animal worlds, students can choose to create their own animal world. In creation mode, they can choose animals and place them in an empty world. They can also set the size of animals as they prefer by using two controllers to perform size change interaction.

Voice Command

The VR Animals application supoorts several simple voice commands. For example, in 360° video playback, students can use voice command "Main Menu" to exit video playback and go back to main menu if they want. The voice command "Main Menu" can be used at almost every scene. Also, there is another "Go back" voice command can be used to go back to the previous scene.

Watch 360° Videos

For each animal, there will be a 360° Video in the introduction panel for students to watch. During video playback, students can play, pause, fast-forward, fast-backward as well as jump to a certain part of the video using the playback control panel.

Teleportation to Move

Given the vast area of the environment, students can use teleportation to navigate in the world supplementary to walking.
